# Module 6: Monitoring, Reporting and Future Trends of ESG

## Module’s Goal

This module helps learners monitor ESG implementation through meaningful indicators and KPIs, report ESG performance credibly, including key EU reporting principles, and use innovation and future-trend scanning to continuously improve ESG practices.

Professionals, managers and operational staff in SMEs and organisations involved in CVET who need to measure ESG progress, coordinate data collection across functions, and communicate ESG performance to internal and external stakeholders.

# Unit 1: ESG Indicators and KPIs: Measuring what matters

## Unit 1 – Introduction

ESG indicators and KPIs are the foundation of effective sustainability management. Without clear and relevant metrics, organisations risk losing control over their ESG commitments or focusing on what is easy to measure rather than what truly matters.

This unit helps learners understand how to move from abstract ESG goals to concrete, measurable indicators that support accountability, decision-making and continuous improvement.

## ESG Indicators

## What are the ESG indicators?

ESG stands for Environmental, Social, Governance.

ESG shall improve the identification of long-term returns for investors and benefit society by influencing the behaviour of companies.

The assessment of companies is carried out through indicators that measure the performance of organisations from the perspective of environment, society and governance.

ESG practices generate benefits that go beyond the social and environmental sphere, optimising the company’s overall performance and adding value to businesses.

The ESG indicators can be standardised in these dimensions:

## Environmental Dimension

To integrate the Environmental Dimension into your ESG strategy, you should refer to the categories mentioned in the chart above and the following explanations.

## Emissions Category

- Refers to your use of greenhouse emissions and other environmental emissions that affect water, air and soil quality.
- The emission score assesses your company’s commitment and effectiveness in reducing environmental emissions.

## Resource Use Category

- Refers to your use of natural resources, including water, energy and non-renewable energies.
- The score reflects on your ability and performance to reduce energy, water, material consumption and finding more eco-efficient solutions.

## Innovation Category

- Refers to your innovation practices, such as investments in product design and innovation, research and development of environmental technologies and solutions, use of sustainable materials, technologies of reducing waste and digitization.
- The score assesses your ability to reduce costs and environmental burdens for its customers.

## Social Dimension

To integrate the Social Dimension into your ESG strategy, you should refer to the categories mentioned in the chart above and the following explanations.

## Workforce Category

- Refers to diversity, inclusion, employee satisfaction, turnover, health and safety at work.
- The score measures the effectiveness of your company in terms of practices and policies related to employees.

## Product Responsibility Category

- Refers to the responsibility of organising in relation to social impacts of your products or services, including product safety, customer satisfaction and data protection.
- The score reflects on the ability of your company to develop quality goods and services with social responsibilities.

## Human Rights Category

- Refers to child labour, forced labour and freedom of association.
- The score measures the effectiveness of your company in terms of respect for fundamental human rights conventions in its process and throughout the supply chain.

## Community Category

- Refers to your impact and initiatives in communities where it operates, including practices of corporate social responsibility and community engagement, such as relationship with the community and charitable actions.
- The score measures company’s commitment to community.

## Governance Dimension

To integrate the Governance Dimension into your ESG strategy, you should refer to the categories mentioned in the chart above and the following explanations.

## Management Category

- Refers to your functionality of the corporate governance structure, including the composition of board of directors and executive compensation.
- The score measures your commitment and performance in adopting best corporate governance practices.

## Shareholder Category

- Refers to the transparency and disclosure of relevant information to shareholders, shareholder rights, auditing and control mechanisms.
- The score deals to effectiveness of your company in its relationship with its shareholders and use of anti-takeover mechanisms.

## CSR Strategy Category

- Refers to your company’s practices in relation to corporate social responsibility (CSR), including code of ethics, anti-corruption, bribery policies and capital structure.
- The score measures your company’s commitment to communicate in how it integrates economic, social, environmental dimensions into decision-making processes.

## How to use Key Performance Indicators

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) enable companies to measure their progress towards sustainability and tell us about their environmental, social and economic impact.

A useful indicator is not “any number you can collect”. It should connect to:

1. Your material topics — what truly matters for your impacts and your risks or opportunities.
2. Your strategy — what you are trying to improve.
3. Your decisions — what managers will do differently because of the data.

## Practical step-by-step mini-method

1. Start from one ESG objective, for example: “reduce energy consumption”.
2. Define what success looks like, for example: “-15% kWh per unit produced”.
3. Choose 1–2 indicators, leading and lagging where possible.
4. Define data source, owner and frequency: who collects, from where, how often.
5. Decide thresholds: what triggers action.

## What is a KPI and how to implement KPIs

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) help companies measure progress towards sustainability and understand environmental, social and economic impact.

In order to implement KPIs in your organisation, you need to:

- Select KPIs that fit your company context and industry.
- Use KPI evaluation to get quantitative and qualitative feedback that informs strategy.
- Use non-financial KPIs to measure sustainability performance beyond profit.

Focused KPI routines improve performance, long-term success and sustainability.
